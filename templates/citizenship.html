Becoming a Citizen of UCDS

The United Communist Democratic States welcomes individuals who share our vision for a harmonious and prosperous society. Citizenship in UCDS is a privilege that comes with both rights and responsibilities, all designed to foster a strong, unified, and progressive nation.

Eligibility and Application

To inquire about citizenship and learn about the application process, please use the form below. General eligibility may include:

  • Agreement with the core principles of UCDS.
  • Commitment to active participation in communal life and democratic processes.
  • A willingness to contribute positively to the nation's development.

Rights of a UCDS Citizen

As a citizen of the United Communist Democratic States, you are entitled to:

  • Participate in democratic elections and referendums.
  • Hold public office within the government.
  • Access communal resources and national services.
  • Enjoy freedom of speech, assembly, and conscience, provided they align with communal harmony.
  • Protection under UCDS law and the national charter.

Responsibilities of a UCDS Citizen

With rights come responsibilities, which are vital for the continued success of our micronation:

  • Uphold the principles and laws of the UCDS.
  • Contribute actively to the community and national projects.
  • Participate in democratic processes and civic duties.
  • Respect the diversity and communal spirit of all citizens.
  • Defend the nation's values and integrity.

Oath of Allegiance

Upon becoming a citizen, individuals are expected to affirm their commitment to the United Communist Democratic States through a symbolic oath:

"I, [Your Name], solemnly pledge my allegiance to the United Communist Democratic States, to its principles of collective prosperity and democratic liberty, and to its people. I vow to uphold its laws, contribute to its progress, and defend its ideals, for the common good and the advancement of our shared future."
