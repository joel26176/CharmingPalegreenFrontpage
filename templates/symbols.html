National Symbols of the UCDS

The symbols of the United Communist Democratic States encapsulate our history, values, and aspirations for a brighter future. They are emblems of unity, labor, and the collective will of the people.

The National Flag

The flag of the UCDS features a deep red field, symbolizing the blood shed in the struggle for liberation and the fiery revolutionary spirit of our people. In the upper hoist corner, a golden five-pointed star represents the unity of the five continents and the bright future of communism, while a stylized hammer and sickle below it signify the unbreakable alliance of workers and peasants. The golden color of the star, hammer, and sickle represents prosperity achieved through collective labor.

The National Emblem

Our national emblem depicts a stylized globe encircled by sheaves of wheat, symbolizing global solidarity and abundant production. Above the globe, a radiant golden sun rises, signifying the dawn of a new era. A red star is prominently featured at the top, and a banner at the bottom proclaims our national motto: "All Power to the People, All Land to the Toilers."

The National Anthem

The national anthem, "Hymn of the United Peoples," is a powerful ode to unity, struggle, and the vision of a classless society. Its stirring melody and lyrical call to action inspire solidarity and revolutionary zeal among all citizens.

The National Flower: The Red Carnation

The vibrant red carnation is the national flower of the UCDS, chosen for its historical association with the workers' movement and its striking color, which mirrors the revolutionary red of our flag. It symbolizes courage, love, and comradeship.
